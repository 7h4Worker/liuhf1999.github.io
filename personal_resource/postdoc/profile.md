Wearable BCI researcher with a foundation in VR-based SSVEP interaction, focusing on how visual paradigms, system design, and neural decoding jointly shape the efficiency of human–machine communication. My work integrates the development of VR paradigms, multimodal EEG acquisition systems, and real-time decoding algorithms to improve the bandwidth, robustness, and user comfort of noninvasive BCIs. I have built several end-to-end platforms—from wireless and FPGA-based EEG devices to VR/AR neural interaction systems—and published in IEEE JBHI, Exploration, Frontiers in Neuroscience, and Journal of Neural Engineering.

My research vision is to enhance neural information throughput and extend VEP-based interfaces into broader wearable and embodied settings. I am particularly interested in how soft and skin-integrated electronics, multimodal sensing, and edge-level processing can complement VR/AR neurointerfaces, enabling more continuous, scalable, and digital-twin-ready representations of human physiological states. This direction naturally connects my background in neural interaction with the emerging landscape of next-generation wearable electronics and Internet-of-Brains technologies.
